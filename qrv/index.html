The Project

The Reasonable Deviations project is a statistical study of the anthropic bias effects.

Selection bias is a distortion of evidence or data that arises from the way that the data are collected. If the selection bias is not taken into account then any conclusions drawn may be wrong.

Any data collected by any known observer have a selection bias - the anthropic bias.

The goal of the project is to test a hypothesis that the observed distribution of the random number sequence, generated by the true random number generator may deviate from normal under the observation selection effect. The secondary goal is to develop a series of tests that and set upper (or lower) limits on the observation selection effects (anthropic bias) for these tests.
